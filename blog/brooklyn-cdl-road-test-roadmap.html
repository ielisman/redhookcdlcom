CDL Road Test Roadmap

CDL Road Test Roadmap

  • Yefim
  • Apr 25, 2022

We already invested on one of our main pages called CDL Process to explain you about CDL Process or Road Map to get to CDL Road Test. We will take an initiative to explain it here in a freestyle format.

Here is the journey from A to Z below, please enjoy the ride!

Commerical Learner's Permit

First, you will need to pass a written test. If you do pass it, you will get a Commercial Learner Permit or CLP. Let's say you want to have an extra speciality, for example driving Hazardous Materials (HazMat) or driving the Tank Vehicles or Doubles/Triples. For that you will need to study separately and take separate tests. You can take those tests at the same time when you take CLP test. $10 DMV fee covers it. If you want to take those tests later, it will be 5 bucks per test. There are certain rules for Hazmat where you need to take your fingerprints and School Bus where you need to take additional practical test. Please read the Commercial Driver's Manual for more details. It should be chapters 7-10 for the time being. Written test has three parts: general knowledge, air brakes, and combination. Study well, especially general knowledge!
You must have NY State regular driver's license or a valid CDL license from another state to take this test.

ELDT

Take ELDT Classes. That's a federal requirement. Can't take Road Test without it. If you have a valid and extended Permit received before Feb 7, 2022 - you don't need to take ELDT but that's a rarity now. Our school will need you to register with DMV for ELDT. You can take it in our school or elsewhere.

Driving Lessons

Take at least 10 driving lessons. Take less than that and you have a very high chance to fail the Road Test. Most of the people are trying to save and eventually end up paying more. Why? Each time you fail, you will need to book a truck for the road test again and it is not cheap. When people fail, they realize they didn't invest enough into the lessons and the learning process. So they keep taking those classes again! Don't make mistake of many. It is true for any CDL School!
You will need to get a very good grip of Pre-Trip Inspection, get comfortable with Basic Control Skills such as Straight Line Backing, left or right Offset Back, and driver side or conventional Parallel park.
And apparently, when taking On-Road Driving inspectors will look at your Overall Performance and General Driving Behavior. Most of the students are nervous during the Road Test, therefore it is essential to get a good grip of all the skills.
While taking lessons, we will suggest you a date for the Road Test. DMV charges $40 to schedule road test, so you need to pay that fee. In case, you will need to re-schedule it, depending on circumstances we might find an early slot for you and if not both you and our staff, will try to arrange you another date. Often, you will need to call DMV because online system does not have the same ability as phone operator has. Please re-schedule at least 3 days prior to the Road Test, otherwise you might need to pay $40 again. And let us know ASAP since we will need to re-arrange our trucks as well.

Medical Certification

If you are planning to drive interstate, i.e. Non-Excepted Interstate (NI) which is the case for the most, you will need to clear K Restriction. For that, you will need to pass USDOT Medical Examination and receive USDOT Medical Examiner's Certificate. Get any doc from the Nationaly Registry We have our doc that we sent our students to; he is pretty good one and charges a decent price: Dr Leonid Toprover @ 207 Ocean Pkwy, Brooklyn, NY 11218. Call him @ 1-718-633-3823

Road Test

Finally, when you are ready for the Road Test, ensure you get a good sleep the night before, arrive early and make sure a valid NY Driver License and a Commercial Learner's Permit are with you. The first part of the test is Pre-trip Inspection. If you pass it, you will go to the Basic Controls Skills Test and the On-Road Driving. If you don't pass, examiner won't send you further, so you will know right away. In this case, you will need to book a new date for the Road Test and pay new $40 DMV fee. If you don't pass Basic Controls Skills and On-Road Driving, you don't need to pay $40 fee. You will need to schedule another Road Test date and only pass the part you didn't pass.

And at last

Once you pass an exam, please call our school and instructor. We are always happy to hear good news and we truly care about our students. Each time you teach someone, as a teacher you do connect with the student on the personal level. We also do value reviews about our company, so we might send you a link to write it down. That's all folks. Contact us if you have any specific questions!
