CDL Process

Read NYS DMV CDL Steps

CDL Steps Summary

Commercial Learner Permit

  1. STEP 1 - have a NY State driver's license or a valid CDL from another state!
    You will need NY State Class D, Class E, or Non-CDL C Driver's License or a valid CDL license from another state.
    Use DMV Form ID44 to transfer a Class D, E, and non-CDL C driver's license from another state to NY.
  2. STEP 2 - get NY State Commercial Driver's Manual and study for the written test!
    Get the manual online or at a local DMV.
    Form MV-500C (and manual's Section 1) describes classes, restrictions, and endorsements of CDLs.
    Study the sections of the manual that apply to the type of CDL you need.
    For truck driving studies, the most common ones are sections 2, 3, 5, and 6. For a permit, you need to pass the written test.
    The test has three parts: general knowledge, air brakes, and combination! General knowledge is the most comprehensive part.
    Please study for separate tests for specific endorsements such as Hazmat, Passenger, School Bus, etc. Ask us if you need any help!
  3. STEP 3 - apply for a commercial learner permit at a DMV office!
    Arrive at your local DMV office! Fill out the form MV44 and pay a $10 fee for the written test. For other special endorsements, this fee covers extra tests that you may take simultaneously as a written test.
    After that, each new test is $5 in case you need other endorsements.
    Please Note: report driver's licenses you had in other states for the last ten years.

Driving Lessons and ELDT

  1. STEP 4 - pay $40 Road Test Fee!
    Once you get the Commerical Learner's Permit, it is good for 6 months. It can be extended if necessary.
    There is a fee of $40.00 to take the CDL road test (skills test). You can pay the road test fee when the DMV office issues your commercial learner permit, or pay it online.
    You must pay the fee before you can schedule your road test. The earlier, the better!
  2. STEP 5 - ELDT Entry Level Driving Training!
    Effective February 7, 2022: If you are applying for a CDL for the first time or adding a Hazmat, Passenger, or School Bus endorsement for the first time, you must complete entry-level driver training (ELDT) prior to taking a CDL road test. Hazmat ELDT is required prior to taking the Hazardous Materials knowledge test. The training provider must be registered on the FMCSA Training Provider Registry. Go to the Entry Level Driver Training (ELDT) FAQ for more information about the ELDT requirement.
    Our ELDT fee is $200. We will enroll you in the ELDT classes and register it with DMV. ELDT is taken in parallel with driving lessons.
  3. STEP 6 - Class A Driving Lessons, Commercial Driver's Manual
    Driving Lessons and Commercial Driver's manual will prepare you for Class A Road (Skills) Test.
    Our instructors will give you all required knowledge including hints and tips about examiners at your road test's location. The Road Test has three parts: Pre-Trip Inspection, The Basic Controls Test, and On-Road Driving Test.
    • Pre-Trip Inspection includes an engine start, an in-cab-inspection, and an inspection of the entire vehicle or only a portion of the vehicle which your CDL examiner will explain to you.
      Please Refer to Section 11 of Driver's Manual
    • Basic Control Skills Test includes Straight line backing, Offset back (left or right), Parallel park (driver side or conventional). Alley docking is not used. For the last two items, you can check your vehicle position twice. For straight line backing only once.
      Please Refer to Section 12 of Driver's Manual
    • On-Road Driving Test includes Specific Driving Maneuvers
      (Starts, Stops, Turns, Intersections, Crossings, Curves, Lane changes, Expressway/City Driving, etc.)
      and Overall Performance and General Driving Behavior (Signals, Steering, Signs; Clutch, Gear, and Break usages, etc.).
      Please Refer to Section 13 of Driver's Manual
    Manual vs Automatic Transmissions - manual Transmission drivers can operate trucks with both Manual and Automatic Transmissions. E Restriction in this case is be removed from license. In general such drivers are more valuable and getting paid better. Trucks with manual Transmission are cheaper, and usually has better fuel economy. That allows hiring companies to save more. However, automatic trucks are getting more popular with students since it is easier to get trained.

DMV Medical Certification

Road Test

  1. STEP 7 - Schedule Road Test and Pass Medical Examination!
    • Schedule Road Test at our school as early as possible. You can schedule it yourself online!
      Please note that you can’t schedule an appointment for a date that is less than 14 days after receiving permit.
      Please note that due to high demand wait line can exceed a month. Our school will do our best to accomodate you early.
    • Reschedule exising road test at least 3 days prior your scheduled time or you will lose $40 DMV Road Test fee per their policy. Notify us immediately for any changes, so we can accomodate a best possible day for your next road test.
    • Pass USDOT medical examination anytime before road test for interstate driving, unless you are planning to have medical exemption A3. Once passed K Restriction will be removed from your new CDL and you will get USDOT Medical Examiner's Certificate. Please read more here You can choose any certified medical examiner from the National Registry.
      We recommend Dr Leonid Toprover. Address: 207 Ocean Pkwy, Brooklyn, NY 11218. Phone: 1-718-633-3823
  2. STEP 8 - Take Road Test!
    • The night before the road test, prepare NYS Non-CDL Driver License and Commerical Learner's Permit - ensure they are both valid and not expired. Prepare comfortable shoes and clothing. Go to sleep early if possible. 30 min before bedtime, take a warm shower and drink any relaxing soothing tea, such as a camomile tea.
    • At the day of the exam, drink a peppermint tea or any other relaxing soothing tea. You can take one with you in a thermos.
      Ensure you have NYS Non-CDL Driver License and Commerical Learner's Permit. Leave and arrive at the exam early.
    • During the exam, keep calm, relax and listen to examiner's question carefully. Answer and drive per your instructor's training.
    • If you pass Pre-Trip Inspection, your examiner invite you for Basic Control Skills Test.
      Otherwise, you will need to reschedule your road test for $40 DMV fee.
    • If you pass Basic Control Skills and On-Road Driving Tests, usually you will get an answer the same day later.
      Please call us and share great news with your instructor - they really want you to succeed and always happy to hear it.
      Otherwise, you will only need to reschedule the part you didn't pass. In this case, you don't need to pay $40 fee.
