CDL Process

Read NYS DMV CDL Steps

DMV Steps Summary
Step 1: Get Commercial Learner Permit
  1. A. Have NY State driver's license or a valid CDL from another state!
    That's a rule to apply for CDL Permit. Use DMV Form ID44 to transfer a non-CDL license from another state.
  2. B. Get NY State Commercial Driver's Manual and study for the written test!
    Get the manual online or at a local DMV. Form MV-500C describes classes, restrictions, and endorsements of CDLs. Study the sections of the manual that apply to the type of CDL you need. Ask us if you need help!
  3. C. Apply for a commercial learner permit at a DMV office!
    For a permit, you need to pass the written test. Fill out the form MV44.
    The test has three parts: general knowledge, air brakes, and combination!
    You need to pay a $10 fee. It covers extra tests that can you can take at the same time, such as Hazmat. After that, each new test is $5 in case you need other endorsements.
    Please Note: report any US state where you had a driver's license for the last ten years.

Step 2: Meet the Federal medical examination requirements
  1. A. For interstate driving, pass USDOT medical examination
    Based on Federal regulations for specific commercial driving, USDOT Medical Examiner's Certificate is required. If you plan to drive in states other than NY, you will need this certificate. Please read more over here
